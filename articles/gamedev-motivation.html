How to Regain Motivation for Game Development

November 8, 2021

So, these past few months have been quite busy, and extremely fun, for me. Moving into college, meeting new people, doing clubs and learning new hobbies, homework, essays, and newspaper illustration work.

I've been enjoying myself, but on the transition I must've left something behind because I can't quite find it anymore:

motivation

Don't get me wrong, I'm not deprived of motivation. Every minute I can spare I've spent on my hobbies, but these are new hobbies: rock climbing, digital illustration, robotics, wildlife photography, etc.

And everytime I look at this game project, one that I'm still excited about and know I won't give up on, I feel guilty. Guilty for not giving a second of my time to it.

I suppose we ended on a bad note, me and the project. Fighting with Unity over how far I could bend its animation system to my will left a bad taste in my mouth. I just wanted to make it easier to import new, flowing animations into my game. I needed some editor scripts to make it easier, but unfortunately Unity has a very barebones system in place in terms of allowing its end-user programmer to play with the internals of its AnimatorControllers and whatnot.

But... that is beside the point. The point is that I need to, I want to, get motivation to work on this project again.

Everytime I open the project file, without having done anything for the past two months, I just sit there and wonder where I last left off. And then, I scan the code, press play and move the player around a bit. In the end, I really don't do anything. It's not that I don't know what I should work on next, it's simply I don't want to work on what is next. I'm so excited by the premise, but can't bring myself to sit down with the project for a couple hours and just hammer things out.

So, how do I get out of this rut?
