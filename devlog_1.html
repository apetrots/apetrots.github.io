Scavenger
Dev Log #1: An Introduction
3/8/21 - 3/14/21

Scavenger is a pretty self-explanatory title for a 2D platformer game I'm developing. The core loop of the game is just that: scavenging in a world that has undergone extensive destruction following their technological peak. The destruction is not total, and there are still remnants of old civilization, but they have morphed and changed in wild, near unrecognizable ways. The game is mostly focused around the remains of a massive futuristic farming complex and its dangerous outskirts. I'll keep the information sparse about the world and its lore, so as to keep a few secrets up my sleave, but that is the general idea. Now, onto what I've been working on in these first weeks of set-up. Here's the one's thats been taking up a lot of that time -- and giving me headaches on occassion: pathfinding. Platformer pathfinding, where the AI can jump, drop, and do everything the player can do, is complex -- way more complex than I initially thought it would be. I have finished developing the basics -- meaning, it's still a janky mess -- but the development of pathfinding and AI will be a project-long iterative process. I'll write up a quick article on how I set up my platformer pathfinding, though I promise you my system is entirely hacky, using the same fake-it-till-you-make-it ideology I use in all my code. I also did some work on custom rope physics. And yeah... the ol' Verlet integration simulation works, until I have to put collisions in and my current system sends rope nodes spontaneously flying into the stratosphere. The main trouble I'm having is how to constraint nodes to be a certain distance to each other while, at the same time, colliding with ground and entities in a such a way to slide along the surface.
