Background

Hello! This summer I decided to take the opportunity between graduating college and hopping into full-time employment to take a long and winding trip to Europe. Initially I had plans to do Workaway's and other gigs to get to know people and practice various crafts while roaming around. To forego the laptop and any other electronics other than a app-barren phone -- to bring the sketchpad and sit for a while inspecting the sights and sounds.

Given this is my first time travelling to Europe I wanted to ensure that I got the experience I was looking for. I've recently been engrossed with the idea of the Wanderschaft (Wanderjahre or "Journeyman years"), a German word for the years of being a wandering tradesman that a newly graduated apprentice undertook in the era of guild-controlled trades in the Middle Ages/Renaissance era (namely in Germany, where it still is a thing in spots!) After completing formal training as an apprentice, it was thought they could practice their craft by roaming around and looking for work -- finding all kinds of clients and work. In addition, they could find the areas that would be best (and most in-need) to settle down for work.

So, when my Dad asked me if I was going to find someone to learn woodcarving from while I was in my month-and-a-half-long trip in Europe I realized that was that element I had been missing. I immediately started looking for a course or program, scouring the internet. I'm happy to report that I believe I quite honestly found the best one I could have. Drevo Ozije (translating to "wood coming to life") is a Czech wooden marionette/carving school created in 2007 by Vladmir Sosna, a former set designer, puppeteer, and carver at Alpha Puppet Theater (for 23 years). Recently they've been holding courses in English with the help of Valerie Meiss, a puppeteer and builder from US who is based in the Czech Republic.

At first, when I emailed I found out the course was filled... I was dissapointed but moved on, trying to find other opportunities. Thankfully, soon after I received an email that a person had to unfortunately drop the course and they needed to fill their spot immediately! I jumped on that pronto and I was in -- a 10 day intensive marionette carving course in a small village in western Czech Republic.

So! That's the background, the course starts on July 10th and so right now I have to get a concept and technical drawings for the wooden marionette I'm making together.
